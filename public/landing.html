سرد · the flow of the story

Meeting notes that narrate themselves.

Sard listens so you can stay present. It transcribes in your browser, writes structured notes with your own local AI, and turns conversations into decisions, tasks, and memory your team actually trusts. Every claim is cited to the exact moment it was said.

Start free, no account needed Self-host the backend

Works with any meeting in a browser tab, in-person recordings, or uploaded audio. No bots joining your calls.

Private by architecture: audio never leaves your machine. Whisper runs on-device, AI runs on your hardware via Ollama, LM Studio, Jan, or llamafile.

Tailored to how meetings actually happen.

Capture anywhere, then let Sard write the story of the meeting: summary, key points, decisions, and action items.

🎙️

Record the room or the tab

Mic for in-person meetings. Tab capture for Zoom and Meet in the browser, mixed with your mic. No setup, no bots.

✍️

On-device transcription

Whisper runs inside your browser with an automatic precision fallback, with live progress and a choice of model sizes.

🧾

Notes with receipts

Summaries, decisions, and tasks link back to exact transcript moments. If the meeting did not say it, Sard does not claim it. Owners and deadlines are never invented.

💬

Ask the meeting anything

Meeting Chat answers only from that meeting's transcript. When the answer is not there, it says so honestly instead of guessing.

From one meeting to organizational memory.

Connect your own server and meetings become searchable knowledge, with you in the approval loop.

  • Groups with real context. Projects, clients, and teams each carry structured context, people, and glossaries that make the AI's output specific instead of generic.
  • Memory you approve. Sard suggests durable facts; you approve, edit, reject, or mark them temporary. Conflicts appear as cards. Nothing is ever silently overwritten, and history is versioned.
  • Group Intelligence. Ask questions across a whole group. Answers draw only on approved memory, documents, transcripts, and decisions, with a visible scope indicator.
  • Sharing with boundaries. External links always expire and serve a summary variant that keeps internal context internal.
